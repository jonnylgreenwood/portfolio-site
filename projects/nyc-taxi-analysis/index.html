NYC Taxi Fleet Analysis

A data consulting case study using CRISP-DM and iterative framework loops.

1. Overview

When I discovered the NYC Taxi dataset, I saw it as the perfect playground to demonstrate how a Data Consultant applies the CRISP-DM framework in practice.

This project simulates how I’d work with a client exploring how to enter and compete in the NYC taxi market, from defining business objectives to building analytical solutions that guide real-world decisions.

The focus isn’t just on the data itself, but on the process: translating business strategy into measurable, data-driven outcomes through structured frameworks, transparent ETL, and iterative refinement.

Key Outcomes:

  • Framework applied end-to-end with measurable business framing
  • Scalable, reproducible ETL pipeline
  • Reporting and modelling
  • Iterative improvements

2. Process

The process follows a cyclical framework, starting with the business defining its objectives.

Each iteration through the cycle deepens understanding and capability: as the business matures, the questions become more complex, and each loop may introduce new datasets, analytical methods, or performance metrics to support decision-making.

3. Framework Loop 1 - Core Business Case and Project Setup

3.1 Core Business Case

Objective: The business operates a fleet of 1,000 vehicles, each working 8 hours per day, 5 days per week. The goal is to maximise total revenue by optimising fleet deployment, ensuring vehicles are in the right place, at the right time, to capture demand efficiently.

This first loop focuses on building the analytical foundation: understanding trip patterns, identifying high-demand zones, and developing a baseline for future optimisation.

3.2 ETL Creation

With the business objective defined, the first step was to design a reliable ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) pipeline to process the NYC Taxi dataset efficiently and reproducibly.

This phase covers the Acquire & Explore Data and Enrich & Transform stages of the wider framework.

The pipeline was built around three data layers (Raw, Cleansed, and Curated), each serving a distinct purpose, from storing unmodified source files to producing analysis-ready tables.
