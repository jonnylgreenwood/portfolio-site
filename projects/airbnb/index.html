Airbnb

A data consulting case study using CRISP-DM and iterative framework loops, incl snowflake and dbt.

1. Overview

After formalising working processes into the CRIPSR methodology with dbt, I wanted to use this approach on cloud-based warehousing. For this, I chose to use SnowFlake due to it's popularity in the market and my interest in learning more about it.

This project simulates how I’d work with a client exploring how to enter into the airbnb market.

The focus isn’t just on the data itself, but on the process: translating business strategy into measurable, data-driven outcomes through structured frameworks, transparent ETL, and iterative refinement.

Key Outcomes:

  • Framework applied end-to-end with measurable business framing
  • Scalable, reproducible ETL pipeline
  • Reporting and modelling
  • Iterative improvements

2. Process

The process follows a cyclical framework, starting with the business defining its objectives.

Each iteration through the cycle deepens understanding and capability: as the business matures, the questions become more complex, and each loop may introduce new datasets, analytical methods, or performance metrics to support decision-making.

Tooling Overview: dbt & Snowflake

This project uses Snowflake as the cloud data warehouse and dbt for data transformation and modelling. Together, they enable:

  • A centralised, cloud-based single source of truth
  • Clear separation between raw, cleansed, and curated data layers
  • Reproducible, version-controlled transformations
  • Transparent business logic embedded directly in SQL models
  • Safe iteration as business questions evolve
The result is a structured analytics workflow that supports repeatable analysis, reliable reporting, and incremental decision-making.

3. Framework Loop 1 - Core Business Case and Project Setup

3.1 Core Business Case

Objective: Understand the volume of bookings per region, and the neighbourhoods with the most desirable properties.

This first loop focuses on building the analytical foundation: understanding trip patterns, identifying high-demand zones, and developing a baseline for future optimisation.

3.2 ETL Creation

With the business objective defined, the first step was to design a reliable ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) pipeline to process the Airbnb dataset efficiently and reproducibly.

This phase covers the Acquire & Explore Data and Enrich & Transform stages of the wider framework.

The pipeline was built around three data layers (Raw, Cleansed, and Curated), each serving a distinct purpose, from storing unmodified source files to producing analysis-ready tables.
